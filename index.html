01 — Vertical Lines

Vertical Lines

This photograph uses vertical lines through the flagpole to create a sense of height and strength. I chose this subject because the tall flagpole stands out clearly against the open sky. The image fits the category since the vertical line naturally draws attention upward. One challenge was finding an angle where the pole was centered without distractions in the background. The photo is effective because its simple composition makes the vertical line easy to recognize.

02 — Horizontal Lines

Horizontal Lines

This image uses horizontal lines created by the horizon where the sea meets the sky. I selected this view because the calm ocean provides a clean and balanced composition. It demonstrates the category by emphasizing stability and peacefulness through the long, straight horizon. The challenge was waiting for a moment when the water was clear enough despite the passing boats. The photograph is meaningful because it gives a relaxing feeling while keeping the horizon as the main visual element.

FRAME 03KODAK · CAT. A
03 — Leading Lines

Leading Lines

This photograph applies leading lines using the stone pathway that guides the viewer's eyes toward the cottages. I chose this location because the path naturally leads to the main subject and adds depth to the scene. The image clearly represents the category since the walkway directs attention from the foreground to the background. One difficulty was avoiding people blocking the path while keeping the framing balanced. The photograph is effective because the leading lines create a clear visual flow and make the scene more engaging.

E Closing reflection · GED0007

Reflection

Throughout this course, Art Appreciation, I gained a deeper appreciation for the history and meaning of art beyond what I had previously known. Learning about movements such as the Renaissance, Fauvism, Cubism, Impressionism, and Surrealism showed me that every style reflects the beliefs, emotions, and experiences of its time. I also became more familiar with the elements and principles of art, which helped me understand why certain artworks leave a lasting impression. The photography activities allowed me to apply these concepts in a practical way instead of only studying them in theory. Looking back, the course taught me that art is not simply something to admire but also a way of preserving stories, ideas, and moments that might otherwise be forgotten.

One of the greatest challenges I encountered was dealing with art block, especially during activities that required creativity and originality. There were moments when I doubted my ideas and felt that nothing I created was good enough. Instead of giving up, I learned to slow down, observe my surroundings more carefully, and trust the creative process. Revisiting the lessons from class also reminded me that art does not always have to be perfect to be meaningful. Over time, I realized that overcoming creative struggles became just as valuable as completing the final output because it taught me patience, resilience, and confidence in my own perspective.

Among all the activities, the photography project became the most memorable because it transformed an ordinary moment into something I will always remember. Taking a photograph of my sister's smile made me realize how art can preserve emotions that words sometimes cannot express. What seemed like a simple assignment became a reminder that the people and moments we often overlook can hold the greatest meaning. That experience changed the way I see photography and art as a whole, making me appreciate their ability to capture memories before they quietly become part of the past. As I finish GED0007, I leave not only with new knowledge about art but also with a greater appreciation for the beauty found in everyday life and the memories we create with the people we love.
